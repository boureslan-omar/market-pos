System Overview

What the system does and why it was built for your store

What Is This System?

The Zoughaib Market POS is a custom-built retail management platform running locally on your computer — no internet required. It handles everything from ringing up a sale at the counter to tracking supplier debts, managing stock, and producing end-of-day financial summaries. Multiple staff members can use it at the same time on different devices connected to the same network.

Key Benefits
⚡

Fast Checkout

Scan barcodes or search by name. Cart updates instantly. Supports cash (USD & LBP), card, and credit sales.

💱

Dual Currency

All prices in USD with live LBP equivalent. Exchange rate set once in settings — updates everywhere automatically.

📦

Smart Inventory

FIFO batch stock tracking. Separate handling for bulk items (fruits/veg) and boxed goods. Auto stock deduction on every sale.

🏦

Cash Register Control

Two physical drawers (USD & LBP) tracked in real-time. Every movement logged with source and timestamp.

🤝

Supplier Management

Track what you owe each supplier. Purchase orders, direct purchases, and consignment goods all handled separately.

📊

Financial Reports

Daily, weekly, and monthly profit & loss. Revenue, cost, and margin breakdowns by product, category, or period.

👥

Multi-User Roles

Admin, Cashier, and Stock roles. Each staff member sees only what they need — no accidental access to financials.

🌐

Works on Any Device

Opens in any browser on your local network. Use one PC as server — tablets, phones, and other PCs connect via Wi-Fi.

🔒

Works Offline

No internet dependency. All data stored locally on your machine. Nothing sent to external servers.

System Requirements

Point of Sale (POS)

Processing sales at the counter CASHIER ADMIN

Making a Sale
  • 1

    Click POS in the menu. The sale screen opens with an empty cart on the right.

  • 2

    Add items using the search bar (type name or scan barcode) or browse the product grid on the left.

  • 3

    Use the + / − buttons to adjust quantity. For bulk items (e.g. vegetables), type the exact weight in grams/kg.

  • 4

    If the customer has a loyalty account, search for them by name or phone in the Customer field at the top of the cart.

  • 5

    Click Confirm Payment. A payment dialog appears.

  • 6

    Enter the amount given (USD or LBP) — or leave both at zero for exact payment. Click Complete Sale.

  • 7

    The receipt appears on screen. Click Print to print or share.

Box products: When you add a boxed item, the quantity increases by the units-per-box (e.g. +12 for a box of 12). You can sell individual units too — just type the number directly.
Payment Methods
MethodWhat Happens
Cash (USD)Amount deducted from USD drawer. Change calculated and shown.
Cash (LBP)Amount deducted from LBP drawer. Change shown in LBP.
CardSale recorded; no drawer movement.
CreditSale added to customer's outstanding balance (credit account).
Voiding a Sale
  • 1

    Go to Reports or find the sale in the sales log.

  • 2

    Click the Void button next to the sale. Confirm.

  • 3

    Stock is restored; cash drawer is adjusted; the sale is marked as voided (still visible for audit).

Note: Only Admin users can void a sale after it has been completed.

Adding a New Product
  • 1

    Go to Products and click Add Product.

  • 2

    Enter: Name, Category, Unit (pcs / kg / box), Cost Price, Sell Price.

  • 3

    For box products: enter Units per Box and optionally a Box Sell Price.

  • 4

    Set a Low Stock Alert threshold — the system will highlight the product when stock falls below this number.

  • 5

    Click Save. A barcode is auto-generated. Print it from Products → Print Barcodes.

Stock Management

FIFO Batches

Each purchase creates a stock batch with its cost price. When a product is sold, the oldest batch is deducted first. If you buy the same product at a different price, a new batch is created automatically.

Importing Products

Go to Import Products to upload a CSV file with bulk product data. Useful when setting up the store for the first time or adding a large new category.

Tip: Use Products → Print Barcodes to print barcode labels for any product. Select multiple products and print to a label printer or regular paper.

Purchase Orders

Ordering from suppliers and receiving stock STOCK ADMIN

Purchase Orders (POs) let you formally request goods from a supplier before they arrive. Once the delivery arrives, you "receive" the PO and stock is updated automatically.

Creating a Purchase Order
  • 1

    Go to Purchase Orders and click New PO.

  • 2

    Select a Supplier from the dropdown.

  • 3

    Add items: search for an existing product or type a new product name. Enter Quantity and Estimated Price.

  • 4

    For a new product in a box: set Unit = Box, enter Units per Box. The system creates the product automatically when you receive the order.

  • 5

    Click Save PO. A PO number (e.g. PO-2026-0001) is assigned.

PO Status Flow
StatusMeaningWhat You Can Do
DraftPO created, not sent yetEdit, delete, or send to supplier
SentSupplier has been notified (WhatsApp or print)Change to Confirmed or Cancelled
ConfirmedSupplier confirmed the orderAwaiting delivery
ReceivedGoods arrived and stock updatedView only
CancelledOrder was cancelledView only
Receiving a PO
  • 1

    On the PO list, click the Receive button (appears when status is Confirmed or Sent).

  • 2

    A modal opens showing all ordered items. Adjust received quantity and actual cost if different from the estimate.

  • 3

    Optionally update Sell Prices for items you want to reprice.

  • 4

    In the Payment Settlement section, choose how you paid: Cash from register, Cash from owner, or Pay later.

  • 5

    Click Commit Receipt & Update Stock. Stock updates immediately; a Purchase record is created automatically.

Send via WhatsApp: Click the WhatsApp icon on any PO to open a pre-filled message to the supplier with the full item list.

Purchases & Suppliers

Recording stock purchases and managing what you owe STOCK ADMIN

Recording a Direct Purchase

Use this when goods arrive without a prior Purchase Order (e.g. a supplier walks in, or a quick cash buy).

  • 1

    Go to Purchases and click New Purchase.

  • 2

    Select the Supplier (required). If the supplier is not in the list, add them first via the Suppliers page.

  • 3

    Add line items: search for a product, enter quantity and unit cost.

  • 4

    Choose a Payment Method:

OptionWhat Happens
Deduct from Cash RegisterAmount withdrawn from USD drawer automatically. Supplier balance set to zero (paid on spot).
Owner Paid CashAmount recorded as cash deposit (owner injected money). Supplier balance set to zero.
Pay LaterNo cash movement. Purchase amount appears as outstanding balance in the Suppliers tab.
  • 5

    Click Save Purchase. Stock batches are updated; supplier ledger is recorded.

Suppliers Page

Go to Suppliers to manage supplier accounts and settle outstanding balances.

  • 1

    The main list shows each supplier's current balance (how much you owe them).

  • 2

    Click on a supplier to see their full ledger — every purchase and every payment.

  • 3

    To make a payment: use the Pay Supplier form. Choose USD cash, LBP cash, or bank transfer. Amount is pre-filled with the outstanding balance.

  • 4

    Click Record Payment. The supplier's balance decreases; the cash drawer is updated if cash was used.

Supplier ledger colours: Red entries = purchases (you owe them). Green entries = payments (you paid them). The current balance is always shown at the top.

Cash Register

Tracking money in and out of both drawers CASHIER ADMIN

The Cash Register page is the financial heart of the system. Every cash movement — sales, purchases, expenses, owner injections — is recorded here automatically. You always know exactly how much cash is in each drawer.

Two Drawers

USD Drawer

Tracks all dollar movements. Opening balance, cash sales, purchase withdrawals, expense deductions, and owner deposits all reflected here in real-time.

LBP Drawer

Tracks all Lebanese pound movements independently. LBP sales, LBP expenses, and LBP supplier payments are recorded separately.

Setting the Opening Balance
  • 1

    At the start of the day, go to Cash Register.

  • 2

    Enter the physical cash count in the Set Opening Balance box (USD and/or LBP separately).

  • 3

    Click Set. The system adjusts the running balance to match your physical count.

What Updates the Drawer Automatically
ActionDrawer Effect
Cash sale (USD)USD Drawer +sale amount
Cash sale (LBP)LBP Drawer +sale amount
Purchase paid from cash registerUSD Drawer −purchase total
Owner paid cash for purchaseUSD Drawer +amount (deposit recorded)
Expense (USD)USD Drawer −expense amount
Expense (LBP)LBP Drawer −expense amount
Supplier payment (cash USD)USD Drawer −payment amount
Consignment settlement (owner cash)USD Drawer +amount (owner deposit)
End of Shift
  • 1

    Click End of Shift at the top of the Cash Register page.

  • 2

    Review the shift summary: total sales, cash in, cash out.

  • 3

    Add an optional note and click Close Shift. A snapshot is saved for future reference.

Customers & Expenses

Customer Accounts CASHIER ADMIN

Create accounts for regular customers to track their purchase history and manage credit balances.

Amenities / Consignment

Managing supplier goods sold on your behalf STOCK ADMIN

Consignment (amenities) are products owned by an external supplier but displayed and sold in your store. You keep a percentage of each sale as your market cut, and periodically settle the rest with the supplier.

How It Works
1️⃣

Supplier delivers goods

Add a consignment product under the supplier's name in Products. Set your selling price and the supplier's agreed percentage/amount.

2️⃣

Sale recorded

Each sale of a consignment product is automatically tracked — revenue split between supplier due and your market profit.

3️⃣

Periodic settlement

When it is time to pay the supplier, use the Pay Supplier form to record the settlement and update cash accordingly.

Settling with a Consignment Supplier
  • 1

    Go to Amenities and click on the supplier name from the left panel.

  • 2

    Review the Outstanding Amount (what you owe them based on unsettled sales).

  • 3

    In the Pay Supplier form, enter the amount (pre-filled with the outstanding balance).

  • 4

    Choose the payment method:

OptionEffect
Deduct from cash register (USD)Withdrawn from USD drawer automatically
Owner paid cashOwner's money — recorded as deposit to register
Bank transferNo cash drawer change; ledger updated
  • 5

    Click Record Settlement. The outstanding balance decreases by the amount paid. Partial payments are supported — only the portion paid is settled.

Consignment Reports

Go to Amenities → Reports tab to see revenue, supplier due, and your market cut by supplier or product for any date range.

Daily Routines & Quick Reference

Recommended workflows to follow every day

Opening the Store (Morning)
  • 1

    Count physical cash in both drawers (USD and LBP).

  • 2

    Go to Cash Register → Set Opening Balance and enter the counted amounts.

    Check the Dashboard for any low-stock alerts — reorder items as needed.

During the Day
  • 1

    Use POS for all sales. Always select the correct payment method.

  • 2

    Record any Expenses as they happen (don't wait until end of day).

  • 3

  • 1

  • 2

    Compare the system's USD and LBP balances with your physical cash count.

    Review Reports for today's sales summary and profit.

Technical Setup & Network Access

Connecting multiple devices and keeping the system running

Server Setup (One-Time)
  • 1

    The system runs on a Windows PC with XAMPP installed. This PC is the server — it must stay on during business hours.

  • 2

    Open XAMPP Control Panel and start Apache and MySQL. Both must show green "Running" status.

  • 3

    On the server PC, the system is accessible at: http://localhost/dahdouh

Connecting Other Devices
  • 1

    Find the server PC's local IP address: open Command Prompt and type ipconfig. Look for the IPv4 Address (e.g. 192.168.1.5).

  • 2

    On any other device on the same Wi-Fi, open a browser and go to: http://192.168.1.5/dahdouh (use your actual IP).

  • 3

    Multiple devices can be logged in simultaneously — each user with their own account.

Tip: Set a static (fixed) IP address on the server PC so the address never changes and other devices always connect reliably.
Database Backup
  • 1

    Open http://localhost/phpmyadmin on the server PC.

  • 2

    Select the database pos_minimarket from the left panel.

  • 3

    Click Export → Go. Save the .sql file to a USB drive or cloud storage.

  • 4

    Repeat this daily or weekly. If the PC ever fails, this file can restore all your data.

Troubleshooting
ProblemSolution
Page won't loadOpen XAMPP Control Panel — make sure Apache and MySQL are both Running (green). Restart if needed.
Other devices can't connectCheck that all devices are on the same Wi-Fi. Check server IP with ipconfig. Disable Windows Firewall for the local network.
Forgot admin passwordOpen phpMyAdmin → pos_minimarket → users table → edit the admin row and set a new password hash.
Stock counts seem wrongCheck the Purchases and PO history to verify all deliveries were recorded. FIFO batches update stock automatically.
